Work

My work focuses on how environmental systems, technological infrastructures, and policy frameworks interact—and how these interactions shape long-term societal outcomes.

I work across environmental modelling, data analysis, and research-driven projects, with experience in hydrological systems, environmental monitoring, and applied sustainability initiatives.

My approach combines analytical tools with systems thinking, aiming to understand not only how systems function, but how they evolve under pressure—climate, technology, and governance.

Writing & Research

Alongside technical work, I write essays and research notes exploring broader structural questions—how technology reshapes institutions, how policy adapts, and how environmental constraints redefine development.

This site serves as a structured space for that work: essays, research analysis, and ongoing projects.
